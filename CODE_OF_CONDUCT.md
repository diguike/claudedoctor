# Code of Conduct

## Our standards

We are committed to a respectful, harassment-free project for everyone. Constructive disagreement about evidence, implementation, and product boundaries is welcome; personal attacks, harassment, discriminatory language, doxxing, credential sharing, and instructions intended to evade platform safeguards are not.

Examples of positive participation include:

- Critiquing claims with reproducible evidence
- Declaring uncertainty and correcting mistakes openly
- Respecting privacy in logs, screenshots, and issue reports
- Giving actionable, technically specific feedback
- Helping contributors of different experience levels participate effectively

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions that violate these standards and may temporarily or permanently restrict participation. For a sensitive conduct report, contact the maintainer through the contact methods listed on the [diguike GitHub profile](https://github.com/diguike). Do not include secrets or unrelated personal information.

This Code of Conduct applies in the repository and in public spaces where someone is representing the project.
